Hack GT 11 Best Overall Project Winner:
Motion Tracking Self Defense Education Webapp

OVERVIEW

In September 2024, I had the opportunity to participate in HackGT 11, a 36-hour hackathon hosted by Georgia Tech with ~500 participants. Our team developed Fight and Furious, a webapp that uses computer vision and motion tracking techniques to provide self-defense training. Our project received 2nd place as the Best Overall Project. Check out our project here!

PERSONAL WORK

During the hackathon, I mainly worked on backend development of a Python script that computes the accuracy of the user's motions compared to the instructor sample using MediaPipe's human pose estimation model, OpenCV.

SKILLS

Python, MediaPipe, OpenCV, React, FastAPI, Git, StreamLit
