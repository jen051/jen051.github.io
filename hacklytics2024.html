Hacklytics 2024: GAN Model for Synthesizing Logic Circuits

OVERVIEW

In February 2024, I had the opportunity to participate in my first ever hackathon – Hacklytics, the largest Data Science hackathon in the US. Our team, the Robotic Registers, created Moor Circuit Gen 1(MCG-1), a Graph Generative Adversal Network(GAN) Deep Learning model for generating digital logic circuits. We won 1st place in the Intel Development Cloud category. Check out our project here!

Our model was trained on a dataset consisting of over 50,000 existing digital logic circuits created by Ajay Yadav, one of my teammates. After training, the MCG-1 model could generate viable random digital logic circuits without discontinuities or improper connections.

The MCG-1 model could be beneficial for those researching and developing technology centered around FPGA and ASIC development. The ability to generate a viable random circuit allows for a model that could be further trained to generate a Register Transfer Level (RTL) design from a much higher-level circuit or description. If used properly, this technology could rapidly cut down on the production time associated with developing chips with Very Large Scale Integration that typically takes years to produce as all gates must be hand-placed to be packed into a small package.

PERSONAL WORK

During the hackathon, I mainly worked on backend development of the GAN model and its training loops. As my understanding of the GAN model evolved, I also researched and experimented with different ML models, eventually deciding to continue with the GAN model approach.
